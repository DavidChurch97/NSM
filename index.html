About Us

We

care for a large collection of national and international exhibits, which we present and interpret for a broad audience from all over the world.

We

care for collections of national and international importance, preserving them, interpreting them and making them accessible to as many people as possible.

We

work with museums and communities across Scotland and beyond, introducing our collections of exhibits to a much wider audience who can't physically visit our museums.

Featured Exhibits

African elephant
The African elephant is the largest living land mammal. Adult males weigh up to six tonnes, and eat their body weight in food every 20 days to keep their huge bodies going.
Dounreay nuclear reactor
Dounreay Nuclear Reactor by Kate Williams and John Lloyd was made in 2007. Kiln cost using uranium glass lit with ultraviolet light.
Steward Ford F1 Car
This SF-2 car was driven for part of the 1998 season by Danish driver Jan Magnussen who achieved a best result of sixth in the Canadian Grand Prix. After that race he was replaced by the Dutch driver Jos Verstappen.
mineral giant
This specimen of smoky quartz shows how big mineral specimens can grow. The dark, almost black colour of these crystals is thought to be caused by exposure to natural radiation in the rock.
Quadruplet pacing bicycle
In the 1890s, track bicycle racing was a popular sport. Each competitor rode in the slipstream of a large pacing machine built for three, four or even five drivers. Stamina was vital – some races lasted for hours.
Chamaeleons
Chamaeleon’s can see in all directions because they can move each eye separately.
